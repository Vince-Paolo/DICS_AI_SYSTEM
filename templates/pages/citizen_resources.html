Evacuation Procedures
Flood Evacuation Procedures
⚠️ Warning Signs: Heavy rainfall, rising water levels, unusual river flow
  1. Move to higher ground immediately if water level is rising
  2. Bring essential documents, medications, and valuables
  3. Follow designated evacuation routes posted in your area
  4. Head to nearest evacuation center or safe shelter
  5. Stay in contact with family and authorities
  6. Never attempt to cross flooded areas - "Turn Around, Don't Drown"
Landslide Safety Measures
⚠️ Warning Signs: Ground cracks, tilting trees, unusual sounds from slopes
  1. Avoid areas at base of steep slopes or hills
  2. Stay away from areas showing signs of instability
  3. Keep away from roads passing through landslide-prone areas
  4. If near a slope, move sideways away from landslide path
  5. Listen for rumbling sounds or rockfall
  6. Report unstable slopes to local authorities immediately
Earthquake Safety & Response
🔴 KEY ACTION: DROP, COVER, HOLD ON
  1. Drop to hands and knees immediately
  2. Cover under sturdy desk/table or against interior wall
  3. Hold on until shaking stops (typically 20-60 seconds)
  4. Stay away from windows and heavy objects that might fall
  5. After shaking stops, carefully exit if building is damaged
  6. Account for family members and move to designated shelter if needed
Volcanic Eruption Safety & Evacuation
🔴 KEY HAZARDS: Ashfall, Lava Flow, Pyroclastic Surge, Lahar, Toxic Gas
  1. Monitor PHIVOLCS alerts — evacuate immediately upon Alert Level 4 or 5
  2. Wear N95 mask or damp cloth to protect from ashfall and toxic gases
  3. Evacuate away from river channels — lahars can travel far from the volcano
  4. Seal doors and windows with wet towels if ashfall occurs before evacuation
  5. Do not return home until PHIVOLCS and LGU declare it safe
  6. Bring goggles, long sleeves, and sturdy footwear to protect from ash burns
  7. Never approach lava flows, craters, or active vents under any circumstances
