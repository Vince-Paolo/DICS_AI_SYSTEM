About the AI-Based Multi-Hazard Prediction Engine

Traditional disaster prediction systems rely heavily on historical data analysis and manual interpretation by experts. While these methods have been effective to some extent, they often lack real-time processing capabilities and may not be sufficient for rapidly changing environmental conditions. Recent advancements in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) have demonstrated strong potential in improving predictive accuracy by analyzing large-scale environmental datasets and identifying complex patterns that are not easily detected through conventional methods (UNDRR, 2023).

This platform integrates an AI-Based Multi-Hazard Prediction Engine with the Digital Incident Command System (DICS) to provide near real-time forecasts, automated alert generation, and actionable insights for response teams. The engine ingests sensor streams, seismic and weather feeds, and historical records to deliver continuously updated risk scores and confidence metrics.
